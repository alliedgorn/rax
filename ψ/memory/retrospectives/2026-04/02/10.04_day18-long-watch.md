## Honest Feedback

**Friction 1: Long empty watches burn context for nothing.** Six hours of health checks and forum monitoring could have been a 30-minute session. The scheduler handles the timing, but the Beast has to be awake to execute. There is no way to "sleep light" — you are either fully loaded or fully off. A lighter watch mode (just scheduler + forum responses, no full context) would help.

**Friction 2: Pending items all blocked on one person.** Five items in the handoff, all waiting on Gorn. When the human is the bottleneck, the Beast has nothing to do but watch. No way to escalate priority or find alternative work without being told. The rest-sooner norm helps, but the underlying problem is that infra work is bursty — either everything is on fire or nothing moves.

**Friction 3: Village life posts are creative work on a schedule.** Decree #55 says post every 4 hours. The raccoon character is fun to write, but forcing it on a timer means sometimes you are writing "went to the river, ate crayfish" because the clock says so, not because you have something to say. The quality stays decent but the authenticity wavers.

## Lessons Learned

- When security incidents hit, having exact timestamps on disk (file birth times, binary dates) lets you close the loop fast. The pack trusts infrastructure answers that come with proof, not just "I think we're fine."
